Faculty of Nursing, Mahidol University Opens the "Nursing and Midwifery Clinic"

At the Faculty of Nursing Residence, Mahidol University, Bang Khun Non, to provide health services to the public

On September 21, 2022, the Faculty of Nursing, Mahidol University, held the grand opening ceremony of the "Nursing and Midwifery Clinic" at the Faculty of Nursing Residence, Mahidol University, Bang Khun Non. Associate Professor Dr. Yajai Sitthimongkol, Dean of the Faculty of Nursing, Mahidol University, presided over the opening ceremony. The event was honored by the presence of Dr. Jadej Thammatacharee, Secretary-General of the National Health Security Office (NHSO), who attended and presented the "Warm Community Nursing Clinic" plaque to mark its status as a joint service unit under the universal coverage health scheme. Faculty executives, lecturers, representatives from neighboring organizations, and local residents from Bangkok Noi district joined the ceremony. Free health check-up services by nursing experts were offered at the event, including breast cancer screening, child development screening, blood glucose testing, elderly common symptom management, and mental health screening for all ages, from September 21 to 23, 2022.

The Nursing and Midwifery Clinic was established to be an accessible, fast, safe, and reliable healthcare service unit for the public, aligning with the policies of the National Health Security Office (NHSO) and the professional council. Surveys also indicated that the public wanted care from the Faculty of Nursing, whose nurses are experts in caring for people of all ages. This clinic acts as a co-operating service unit in the National Health Security system, providing primary care, risk screening, disease prevention, health counseling, and referring high-risk or ill individuals to appropriate medical systems to reduce overcrowding in hospitals.

The Nursing and Midwifery Clinic at the Faculty of Nursing Residence, Mahidol University, Bang Khun Non, is open to the public every Monday to Friday from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM, providing health services by specialized professional nursing faculty members.
